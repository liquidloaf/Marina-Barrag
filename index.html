About Marina Barrage

Marina Barrage is built across the mouth of the 350-metres wide Marina Channel (between Marina East and Marina South), and it is Singapore's 15th reservoir, supplying us with roughly 10% of the country's water supply. It was officially opened on the 30th October 2008. It's main function is to function as a dam; to aid water storage and flood control. Marina Barrage also has a catchment Ara of 10,000 hectares, increasing Singapore's water catchment area from half to two-thirds of Singapore's land area. This place is also popular for recreation activities such as water sports, kite flying and picnic.

Special Features

Flood Control

Marina Barrage will open their nine crest gates of 30-metre-wide and 5-metre-high, during periods of heavy rain to prevent flooding, and to release excess water from the rain into the sea when the tides are low. During high tides, seven giant pumps will be used, draining water into the sea. These pumps are capable of pumping an Olympic-sized swimming pool in a minute.

Green Features

When building the Marina Barrage, the Late Mr Lee Kuan Yew wanted the Marina Barrage to be a green place. Hence, the designs and construction of this beautiful architecture revolves around the idea of being environmental friendly.

The double glazed panels reduces heat indoors and reduces the amount of electricity used to operate the air-conditioning systems at Marina Barrage. The solar park contains 405 panels that helps to provide lightings in the gallery and offices in the Marina Barrage. The rooftop also uses 100% recycled plastics, and it provides natural heat for the building. Many more green-features can be found at the link below.

Things to Do

Water Activities

Boating, Kayaking and dragon boating can be done around Marina Barrage as it is unaffected by the tides and the water level are mostly kept constant all year round. This allows fun for people of all ages.

Family Picnic

Moreover, there is a large green space at the rooftop of the barrage, allowing family and friends to enjoy a picnic and chill with their loved ones, or to simply just to fly a kite. Apart from that, many marathons takes place here, in which the participants have to run through Marina Barrage bridge itself.

Watch the Sunrise or Sunset

With the stunning backdrop of downtown Singapore from the rooftop of Marina Barrage, people love to go there during both sunset and sunrise as it provides an amazing view of the sky and the sun. From here, you could also see Marina Bay Sands and Singapore Flyer, making it one of the best spots for an Instagram worthy picture.

Water Fountain Playground

Marina Barrage is also equipped with a water fountain playground, where people of all ages can come together to have a soaking wet fun, especially on a humid day. It consist of an open and shallow wading pool, fountains that light up at night and many playful jets of water. It also has a water jet tunnel and a square water shelter, where people can sit in the water, while being shaded from the sun. A curtain of water flows down from the glass roof of the water fountain.
